Football and car accidents

Apparently, football games prevent car accidents.

We looked at every accident from 2010-2014, and every Barcelona football game during the same period. It turns out that, on average, game days are about 11% safer than non-game days.

Status Days Accidents Daily Rate
No game 1572 40376 25.68
Game day 254 5802 22.84

Maybe it's not really the football...

One could surmise that football matches keep people inside (glued to their television screens) rather than on the road (probably true), or that games take place more on the weekends than weekdays (when there are less accidents generally) . In other words, football itself isn't causing anything, and is simply acting as a proxy for traffic density.

Is this the case? We don't think so.

Then again, maybe it is...

When we dig deeper, we find that the outcome of the match matters more than whether or not there is a game. When the home team wins, there are few crashes. But when Bar√ßa loses, there are even more crashes than on non-game days!

Outcome Days Accidents Daily rate
Loss 26 711 27.35
No game 1572 40376 25.68
Tie 45 1078 23.96
Win 183 4013 21.93
That's kind of crazy, right?

Two theories

What's the reason for this? Two possible theories:

(1) People just get mad and drive worse when their team loses (possible, but unlikely).

(2) People leave venues in a more staggered fashion after a victory than after a defeat (since post-victory parties are more likely than post-defeat parties).

So, does football save lives?

That's probably a stretch. Yes, there are slightly fewer deaths on a winning vs. losing days, but this doesn't quite reach the level of "statistical significance." Perhaps la liga should ban ties?

That said, the reduction in accidents associated with football victories is significant. So, if you're reading this, Messi - put down the computer, and get back to your training!
