The most dangerous intersection in Barcelona

You guessed it: Avinguda Diagonal and Passeig de Gracia.

This technically counts as one intersection, right?

From 2010 to 2014, there were 129 accidents involving 165 victims and 248 vehicles. That's an average of one accident every two weeks.

Yikes.

But everywhere is kind of dangerous

The reality is that, relative to other comparable cities, Barcelona is a dangerous place. Sure, Rome is worse (who's surprised?), but Barcelona's got a lot of cars, a lot of motorcycles, a lot of old roads, and a lot of people trying to get from point A to point B. And all that commotion adds up - to an average of 9,235 accidents per year over the last 5 years, which sums up to more than 25 per day.

And it doesn't appear to be getting any better.

In 2010, there were just a hair over 9,000 accidents. In 2011, that number dropped to 8,809. Since then, however, the number of accidents has climbed by a few hundred per year, peaking at 9,660 in 2014 (the last year for which data are available). 25 a day.

What's behind all these crashes?

Who knows. As consistent as the yearly numbers are, there's a lot of variation by time and day. Afternoon is far worse than morning, for example. Here's a subtitle for the above.
