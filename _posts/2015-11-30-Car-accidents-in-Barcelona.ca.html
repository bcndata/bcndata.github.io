Els barcelonins xoquen. I molt.

Seria molt guai veure tots els accident en un mapa, no? Si cliques AQUÍ podras fer-ho. Tot els accidents de cotxe a Barcelona del 2010 al 2014.

Amb tants accidents, estaras pensant, "com puc evitar aquests merders?". Aquí no trobarás la resposta, però pots començar evitant la intersecció més perillosa de Barcelona...

I la guanyadora és...

Exacte, Avinguda Diagonal amb Passeig de Gracia!

Ja es veu que no és una intersecció senzilla...

Del 2010 al 2014, hi ha hagut 129 accidents amb 165 víctimes en 248 vehicles. Una mitjana d'un accident cada dos setmanes!

Uups!

Però tot és una mica perillós

La realitat és que, comparant amb altres ciutats, Barcelona és especialment perillosa. Sure, Roma és pitjor. (quina sorpresa, no?), però Barcelona té molts cotxes, moltes motos, molts autobusos i molta gent intentant anar de punt A a punt B. Tot això porta a una mitjana de 9.235 accidents per any en els últims 5 anys, el que correspon a més de 25 accidetns per dia.

I no sembla que estigui millorant

El 2010, hi havia només una mica més de 9.000 accidents. El 2011, In 2010, there were just a hair over 9,000 accidents. In 2011, that number dropped to 8,809. Since then, however, the number of accidents has climbed by a few hundred per year, peaking at 9,660 in 2014 (the last year for which data are available).

When do accidents happen?

As consistent as the yearly numbers are, there's a lot of variation by time and day. Afternoon is far worse than morning, for example. In the below chart, you can make out spikes at around 8-9 AM (morning rush hour), 2 PM (afternoon break) and 5-7 PM (evening rush hour). It's odd that the mid-day spike is so much higher than the morning and evening spikes - are that many people on the road at that time?

Weekdays (especially Friday) are far worse than weekends.

When we look at the averages over the course of an entire week, we see that weekdays have a pre-midnight spike (coming home from dinner), which is conspicuously absent on weekends.

It also seems that most of the Friday spike can be attributed to the AM - not PM - rush. Strange...

Like stars in the night sky

Car accidents are a pretty morbid topic. They take far too many lives, and cause countless suffering by those affected.

Data visualization can help us to absorb numbers too large for our primate brains (ie, the tens of thousands of car accidents in Barcelona over the last few years, and the hundreds of associated deaths). Data sources, code, etc.

OpenDataBCN hosts the dataset for traffic accidents managed by the Guardia Urbana. It's a super interesting dataset with a lot more than what was explored here (mortality, number of injuries, etc.).
